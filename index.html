1. The Core Insight

Ripple's products cannot scale under the current architecture. The March 2026 Platform Vision describes Ripple serving Corporates, Banks, and Repo at institutional scale. Closing that gap requires infrastructure built to handle it, not just a larger sales team.

The model that works at scale has three layers: product teams build differentiated user experiences; platform teams own shared infrastructure; operations handles genuine exceptions. Product teams compose features from platform APIs instead of building foundational capabilities themselves. Operations has a bounded scope because the infrastructure beneath it actually works. At Square, three platform teams underpinned every point-of-sale product globally. Each capability, once built, accelerated every product that invoked it.

Ripple's five product teams currently share the same foundational needs, with no shared infrastructure to meet them: compliant identity, real-time banking, financial primitives, blockchain access, reconciliation. Each team built its own version, or relies on manual processes to cover the gap. At current scale this functions poorly. At the scale the Platform Vision describes, it does not function at all. The March 2026 Platform Vision states the fix plainly: Ripple Core owns and operates the infrastructure layer; Prime and Treasury build on top. Five platform teams with clear scope and defined interfaces give RPD, COBO, Custody, OTC, and Stablecoin shared primitives to compose from.

The fundamental thesis
Platform infrastructure is what lets products scale. Product teams that compose from shared primitives ship faster, operate in more markets, and add new customers without compounding operational complexity. Build the platform layer and each new corridor or customer costs less than the last. Skip it and growth compounds the problem instead.

Current State — Capability Ownership (June 2026)
The cost of fragmentation
Red cells are the same capability rebuilt independently across product codebases, each with its own failure modes, none shareable. Amber cells are manual processes substituting for infrastructure that does not exist. Growth makes both worse. In June 2026, a single API date mismatch from a banking partner caused EUR 40K and GBP 10K in customer deposits to fall out of safeguarding reconciliation. The control system was five manual Google Sheets across $1B+ in payment volume. That is the scale ceiling for amber cells: they hold at low volume, then create regulatory exposure when the product grows into stricter markets. The platform layer is what removes the ceiling.

3. Reference Architecture — The Platform Model

At Square, platform teams owned the shared infrastructure and product teams composed features from it. Three teams underpinned every point-of-sale product globally. Ripple needs the same structure.

Square — Platform Teams

1
Trusted Identity
Compliant onboarding flows, unified customer identity, KYC/KYB verification, authorization rules. Every product invoked this. None built their own.
2
Payment Platform
All acquirer integrations, payment processing, financial primitives. Products called the API. No bespoke payment logic in product code.
3
Banking Connections
Bank integrations, ACH/wire/RTP payout rails, balance monitoring, partner management. One team, one integration, all products.

Ripple — Proposed Equivalents

1
Trusted Identity
Unified customer identity, compliant onboarding, real-time sanctions/AML (Helios), Travel Rule compliance. Replaces fragmented KYC across products.
2
Money Engine
10 canonical financial operations, double-entry ledger, automated reconciliation. The Financial Primitives team's proposal. The composition layer for all financial workflows.
3
Banking Connections
Bank APIs, real-time balance monitoring, inbound/outbound RTP rails, payout partner management. Replaces Zeus and all fragmented bank integrations.

Ripple's model goes further than Square's because the product surface includes blockchain-native capabilities. Two additional platform teams complete the stack: Blockchain Platform (XRPL, wallets, custody infrastructure) and Tokenization Platform (RLUSD issuance, token lifecycle). These five teams own the full infrastructure that RPD, COBO, Custody, OTC, and Stablecoin compose from.

5. Platform Team Definitions

Each platform team owns one capability domain, exposes a clean API, and is responsible for its reliability and scale across all products. Everything in this list belongs to platform, not to the product teams building on top.

Trusted Identity
Square analog: Trusted Identity · Platform Vision Priority #1
Owns the unified customer identity layer. From initial onboarding through ongoing compliance checks on every transaction, all products invoke this team's APIs. None build their own KYC logic.
Owns
Compliant onboarding flows (KYC/KYB) · Unified identity graph (replaces Salesforce as backend) · Real-time sanctions & AML screening (Helios) · Authorization rules & daily limits · Travel Rule compliance · Identity metadata for Money Engine gating
Replaces / retiresFragmented KYC across RPD, Custody, OTC · Salesforce in production flows · Manual identity lookups in RLUSD redemption
Money Engine
Financial Primitives team · RAIL rebuild
The composition layer for all financial workflows. Ten canonical operations exposed as a clean API, with a shared double-entry ledger as the single source of truth. Products describe workflows in operation sentences; Money Engine executes them atomically.
Owns
Transfer · Exchange · Settle · Net · Lock · Release · Pledge · Issue · Redeem · Allocate · Shared double-entry ledger · Automated reconciliation · Real-time financial position · XRPL routing by default · Automated reversal on failure
Replaces / retires4-system manual reconciliation · BillingPlatform in recon path · False-FAILED payment state · No authoritative financial position · Zeus (for financial state)
Banking Connections
Square analog: Banking Connections · Platform Vision Priority #3
Owns all integrations to the traditional banking system, inbound and outbound. When a product team needs to move money on a fiat rail, this team's API handles it. Product teams call the API; they don't build bank integrations.
Owns
Bank APIs (GTreasury + direct) · Real-time balance monitoring (push/webhook, replacing Zeus polling) · Inbound RTP rails (FedNow, PIX, FPS, SEPA Instant) · Outbound payout partner management (JPMC, Volt, Thunes, Form3, Tipalti) · ACH, wire, SEPA · Automated failover routing · Partner onboarding automation
Replaces / retiresZeus polling (fails silently) · Static routing with no failover · 10-12 week manual partner onboarding · Siloed bank integrations across products · Zand manual treasury sweep
Blockchain Platform
Platform Vision Priorities #2 (Blockchain Abstraction) + #5 (Wallet-as-a-Service)
Owns all blockchain access: XRPL connectivity, wallet infrastructure, custody primitives, and on-chain event monitoring. Product teams work with wallets and accounts as abstract concepts; this team owns the chain-specific implementation.
Owns
Unified XRPL connectivity (replaces fragmented integrations) · Palisade hot wallet layer · Harmonize vault integration · MPC key management · On-chain event monitoring · Multi-chain abstraction (future) · Wallet-as-a-Service API · XRPL DEX execution
Replaces / retiresSeparate blockchain integrations in Custody, Stablecoin, RX · Palisade licensing gap (BitGo CaaS fallback) · Duplicated XRPL SDK usage across teams
Tokenization Platform
Platform Vision Priority #6 · RLUSD team → platform capability
Owns token issuance, lifecycle management, and distribution, starting with RLUSD and extending to any future institutional token. The RLUSD team's product-specific work becomes a reusable platform that any product team can invoke to issue, manage, or redeem tokens.
Owns
RLUSD issuance and redemption · Token lifecycle (mint, burn, freeze, confiscate) · Asset tokenization via MPT on XRPL · Proof of reserve · Distribution management (multi-chain: XRPL + EVM future) · Compliance hooks (OFAC, KYC, AML) · RLUSD as bridge asset for Money Engine Exchange operations
Replaces / retiresRLUSD mint/burn as a manual onboarding bottleneck · No reusable tokenization layer for future assets · Bespoke token logic per product

7. Organizational Structure

Platform teams and product teams are peer organizations under Engineering leadership. Product Operations is the third stream: it manages active gaps during the build-out and routes production signal back to both. As platform coverage expands, Ops moves from gap-filling to exception management.

9. Strategic Alignment — Platform Vision

This org model implements the Platform Vision. The March 2026 Ripple Financial Infrastructure document states it plainly: Ripple Core owns and operates the infrastructure layer for digital assets; Prime and Treasury build on top. These five platform teams are "Ripple Core": the shared infrastructure every product team composes from, and that external institutions can access directly.

The Platform Vision also names three validation anchors: Corporates (money engine, account abstraction, fiat/crypto rails), Banks (asset issuance, RLUSD as the base for bank-issued digital assets), and Repo (custody, settlement, capital markets infrastructure). Each exercises multiple platform teams at once. A shared layer serves all three; per-product builds do not. The five teams here map to the Vision's six coherent actions, with RAIL absorbed into Money Engine.

Platform Vision PriorityPlatform TeamWhat It Delivers
#1 Identity & Access Abstraction Trusted Identity Unified customer identity graph, compliant onboarding, auth primitives. Returns Salesforce to CRM role.
#2 Blockchain Abstraction Layer Blockchain Platform Single XRPL integration for all teams. Retires duplicated integrations in Custody, Stablecoin, RX. Consistent wallet, balance, and event APIs.
#3 Banking Abstraction Layer Banking Connections Standardized bank & payout partner access. Real-time balance monitoring. RTP rail connectivity. Replaces Zeus and all siloed bank integrations.
#4 RAIL (decommissioned → rebuild in-house) Money Engine The composition layer RAIL was intended to be: 10 canonical operations, shared ledger, clean API. The Financial Primitives team's proposal is the right foundation.
#5 Wallet-as-a-Service (Palisade) Blockchain Platform Palisade as the universal hot wallet layer. Migrates Rail wallet functionality. Covers internal Ripple custody use cases. Resolves SCTC licensing gap via unified platform.
#6 Tokenization Engine Tokenization Platform Reusable issuance, lifecycle, and distribution infrastructure. RLUSD as the first proof point, extended to any institutional token (tokenized deposits, MMFs, repo collateral).
The sequencing question
Start with Money Engine and Banking Connections. These two teams address the highest-risk scale blockers: the safeguarding reconciliation architecture that creates regulatory breach risk in strict jurisdictions, the Zeus and spreadsheet deposit attribution that breaks silently as volume grows, payment failover, and false-FAILED state. They also unlock the RTP architecture that eliminates prefunding for RPD. Trusted Identity becomes the priority when COBO scoping starts. Blockchain Platform and Tokenization can be phased in as existing team work is reorganized, not net-new headcount.
