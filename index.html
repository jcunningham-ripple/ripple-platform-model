1. The Core Insight

Ripple's bet is to own the infrastructure layer for digital assets — the layer that connects fiat, markets, and blockchain in a way no single competitor does today. That layer has to be built internally before it can be sold externally, and it can't be built coherently while every product team assembles its own version of the same foundations.

Three layers make that work at scale. Solutions teams compose customer experiences from platform APIs. Platform teams own the shared infrastructure — built so that happy paths automate completely and edge cases surface clearly for operations. Ripple Core Operations — Product Ops, Risk Ops, Finance Ops (with Treasury), and Recon Ops — holds and resolves what the automated path cannot. As platform coverage grows, the scope of exceptions narrows.

Five product teams share the same foundational needs — compliant identity, real-time banking, financial primitives, blockchain access, reconciliation — with no shared layer to meet them. Each team built its own version or left Ops to cover the gap. The March 2026 Platform Vision names the fix: Ripple Core owns the infrastructure layer; Prime and Treasury build on top. Six platform teams — Trusted Identity, Money Engine, Banking Connections, Blockchain Platform, Tokenization Platform, and Exchange Platform — give every product team shared primitives to compose from.

The fundamental thesis
Product teams that compose from shared primitives ship faster, reach more markets, and onboard new customers without multiplying ops complexity. Each new corridor costs less than the last. Without the platform layer, growth compounds the problem instead.

Four ways platform creates value for Ripple, roughly in order of when returns appear:

Product cohesion Every product runs on the same identity layer and settles through the same infrastructure. Customers experience One Ripple — consistent regardless of which product they're in. The clearest near-term reason to make the bet.
Engineering efficiency Multiple teams independently built things that claim to be a ledger. As shared primitives get adopted, engineering time shifts from rebuilding foundations to differentiated product work.
A platform we can sell Sophisticated customers integrate directly with the platform, at the edges of what packaged solutions handle. We're at the start of that journey — but it's part of the thesis.
A bridge to the XRPL A cohesive platform becomes the natural place to layer in XRPL capabilities — routing transactions on-chain, decentralized identities, connecting product work with what's being built on the ledger. Only Ripple can pursue this.

The cost of fragmentation
Red cells: the same capability rebuilt independently across product codebases, each with its own failure modes. Amber cells: manual processes substituting for missing infrastructure. In June 2026, one banking partner API date mismatch dropped EUR 40K and GBP 10K in customer deposits out of safeguarding reconciliation — five manual Google Sheets covering $1B+ in volume were the control system. Amber cells hold at low volume, then create regulatory exposure when the product reaches stricter markets. The platform layer removes that ceiling.

4. Platform Team Definitions

Each platform team owns one capability domain and is accountable for its reliability across all products. None of these capabilities belong in product codebases.

Trusted Identity
Platform Vision Priority #1
Owns the unified customer identity layer. From initial onboarding through ongoing compliance checks on every transaction, all products invoke this team's APIs. None build their own KYC logic.
Owns
Compliant onboarding flows (KYC/KYB) · Unified identity graph (replaces Salesforce as backend) · Real-time sanctions & AML screening (Helios) · Authorization rules & daily limits · Travel Rule compliance · Identity metadata for Money Engine gating
Replaces / retiresFragmented KYC across RPD, Custody, OTC · Salesforce in production flows · Manual identity lookups in RLUSD redemption
Money Engine
Financial Primitives team · RAIL rebuild
The composition layer for all financial workflows. Ten canonical operations exposed as a clean API, with a shared double-entry ledger as the single source of truth. Products describe workflows in operation sentences; Money Engine executes them atomically.
Owns
Transfer · Exchange · Settle · Net · Lock · Release · Pledge · Issue · Redeem · Allocate · Shared double-entry ledger · Automated reconciliation · Real-time financial position · XRPL routing by default · Automated reversal on failure
Replaces / retires4-system manual reconciliation · BillingPlatform in recon path · False-FAILED payment state · No authoritative financial position · Zeus (for financial state)
Banking Connections
Platform Vision Priority #3
Owns all integrations to the traditional banking system, inbound and outbound. When a product team needs to move money on a fiat rail, this team's API handles it. Product teams call the API; they don't build bank integrations.
Owns
Bank APIs (GTreasury + direct) · Real-time balance monitoring (push/webhook, replacing Zeus polling) · Inbound RTP rails (FedNow, PIX, FPS, SEPA Instant) · Outbound payout partner management (JPMC, Volt, Thunes, Form3, Tipalti) · ACH, wire, SEPA · Automated failover routing · Partner onboarding automation
Replaces / retiresZeus polling (fails silently) · Static routing with no failover · 10-12 week manual partner onboarding · Siloed bank integrations across products · Zand manual treasury sweep
Blockchain Platform
Platform Vision Priorities #2 (Blockchain Abstraction) + #5 (Wallet-as-a-Service)
Owns all blockchain access: XRPL connectivity, wallet infrastructure, custody primitives, and on-chain event monitoring. Product teams work with wallets and accounts as abstract concepts; this team owns the chain-specific implementation.
Owns
Unified XRPL connectivity (replaces fragmented integrations) · Palisade hot wallet layer · Harmonize vault integration · MPC key management · On-chain event monitoring · Multi-chain abstraction (future) · Wallet-as-a-Service API · XRPL DEX execution
Replaces / retiresSeparate blockchain integrations in Custody, Stablecoin, RX · Palisade licensing gap (BitGo CaaS fallback) · Duplicated XRPL SDK usage across teams
Tokenization Platform
Platform Vision Priority #6 · RLUSD team → platform capability
Owns token issuance, lifecycle management, and distribution, starting with RLUSD and extending to any future institutional token. The RLUSD team's product-specific work becomes a reusable platform that any product team can invoke to issue, manage, or redeem tokens.
Owns
RLUSD issuance and redemption · Token lifecycle (mint, burn, freeze, confiscate) · Asset tokenization via MPT on XRPL · Proof of reserve · Distribution management (multi-chain: XRPL + EVM future) · Compliance hooks (OFAC, KYC, AML) · RLUSD as bridge asset for Money Engine Exchange operations
Replaces / retiresRLUSD mint/burn as a manual onboarding bottleneck · No reusable tokenization layer for future assets · Bespoke token logic per product
Exchange Platform
Markets & Liquidity Primitives · DEX execution · OTC quote flows
Owns exchange primitives, liquidity management, and market execution across fiat, crypto, and tokenized assets. Product teams invoke this team's APIs for quote, execution, and settlement — none build their own market-making or liquidity management logic. The Exchange Platform is the composition layer for any workflow that touches price discovery, conversion, or liquidity positioning.
Owns
DEX execution on XRPL · OTC request-for-quote (RFQ) and quote management · Liquidity pool management (RLUSD and future assets) · FX rate feeds and conversion primitives · Bid/ask spread management · Market depth and liquidity monitoring · Cross-venue execution routing · Liquidity analytics and reporting
Replaces / retiresBespoke DEX integrations in RPD and OTC codebases · Per-product liquidity monitoring · Manual RLUSD liquidity management by Stablecoin team · No shared exchange primitive across products

6. The Role of Ripple Core Operations

Platform's job is to automate the happy path. Operations holds what doesn't fit — and is the primary signal for what the platform should automate next. Product-aligned pods own exceptions and escalations within their domain. Horizontal functions handle what crosses product boundaries. Every recurring exception is a platform gap; the declining exception rate is the most direct measure of platform progress.

TeamTypeScopePlatform dependency
Payments Operations Product-aligned Exceptions, escalations, and investigations for RPD and COBO Banking Connections (payment monitoring, corridor health); Money Engine (false-FAILED state, automated reversal)
OTC Operations Product-aligned Exception handling and escalations for the trading desk Exchange Platform (DEX execution, RFQ); Money Engine (settlement state)
Stablecoin Operations Product-aligned Exception handling for RLUSD mint/burn, reserve, and distribution flows Tokenization Platform (issuance/redemption API); Banking Connections (safeguarding banking)
Financial Operations Horizontal End-of-day reconciliation, safeguarding checks, break investigation, asset positions, and treasury management Money Engine (shared ledger); Unified Settlements (automated settlement reduces break surface); Accounts Primitive
Risk Operations Horizontal Risk review and ongoing compliance monitoring across all products Trusted Identity (real-time AML/sanctions); FinCrimes platform (Unit21, Helios)
The model working in practice
Unified Settlements went live in Q2 2026. Since then, 60.8% of OTC settlements run through the platform and Jira-based manual handling has dropped to 39.2% — still falling. 58.7% of fiat settlement volume is fully automated. That is the ops exception rate declining as platform coverage expands. The 546 reconciliation breaks and EUR 40K + GBP 10K safeguarding mismatches from Q1 2026 are exactly what Money Engine and Financial Operations address together: the platform closes the gap, Financial Operations holds it in the meantime.

7. Strategic Alignment — Platform Vision

This org model implements the Platform Vision directly. Ripple's bet is to own the infrastructure layer for digital assets — the layer that connects fiat, markets, and blockchain in a way no single competitor does today. You can't sell that externally if you haven't built it internally, and you can't build it coherently when every product team assembles its own version of the same foundations. Ripple Core owns and operates that infrastructure layer; Prime and Treasury build on top. These six platform teams are Ripple Core.

The Vision names three anchors: Corporates (money engine, account abstraction, fiat/crypto rails), Banks (asset issuance, RLUSD as the base for bank-issued digital currency), and Repo (custody, settlement, capital markets). Each exercises multiple platform teams. The first five teams map to the Vision's six coherent actions, with RAIL absorbed into Money Engine. Exchange Platform extends the model for the Repo anchor: market execution and liquidity primitives that capital markets workflows require.

Platform Vision PriorityPlatform TeamWhat It Delivers
#1 Identity & Access Abstraction Trusted Identity Unified customer identity graph, compliant onboarding, auth primitives. Returns Salesforce to CRM role.
#2 Blockchain Abstraction Layer Blockchain Platform Single XRPL integration for all teams. Retires duplicated integrations in Custody, Stablecoin, RX. Consistent wallet, balance, and event APIs.
#3 Banking Abstraction Layer Banking Connections Standardized bank & payout partner access. Real-time balance monitoring. RTP rail connectivity. Replaces Zeus and all siloed bank integrations.
#4 RAIL (decommissioned → rebuild in-house) Money Engine The composition layer RAIL was intended to be: 10 canonical operations, shared ledger, clean API. The Financial Primitives team's proposal is the right foundation.
#5 Wallet-as-a-Service (Palisade) Blockchain Platform Palisade as the universal hot wallet layer. Migrates Rail wallet functionality. Covers internal Ripple custody use cases. Resolves SCTC licensing gap via unified platform.
#6 Tokenization Engine Tokenization Platform Reusable issuance, lifecycle, and distribution infrastructure. RLUSD as the first proof point, extended to any institutional token (tokenized deposits, MMFs, repo collateral).
#7 Exchange & Liquidity Primitives Exchange Platform Shared market execution and liquidity management layer. DEX access on XRPL, OTC quote and RFQ primitives, RLUSD liquidity positioning — composable across all products that touch price discovery, conversion, or exchange workflows. Directly serves the Repo anchor's capital markets requirements.
Sequencing: where to start
Money Engine and Banking Connections first. They address the highest-risk blockers: safeguarding reconciliation creating regulatory breach risk in strict jurisdictions; Zeus and spreadsheet deposit attribution that breaks silently at volume; false-FAILED payment state. They also unlock the RTP architecture that eliminates prefunding for RPD. Trusted Identity becomes the priority when COBO scoping starts. Blockchain Platform and Tokenization phase in as existing team work reorganizes — not net-new headcount.
